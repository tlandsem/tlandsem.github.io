// Privacy

Husk is a personal project, not a commercial service. No ads, no tracking, no analytics.

You don't create an account with a name or email. The app automatically generates a private access key that is stored in your browser on your device.

The only things stored are your reminders (text and deadline), your notification choice, and a push subscription so your device can receive alerts. Your list is private — no other user can see it.

Data is stored on a server and database in the EU (Frankfurt). The data controller is Trine Landsem. For questions or to have your data deleted, get in touch via the contact page. You can also delete your reminders yourself in the app at any time.

// Security

All traffic is encrypted (HTTPS/TLS), and the content of notifications is encrypted. Every request is tied to your own access key, so you only ever reach your own data.

Note: this is a free hobby service. Avoid putting sensitive information (health, passwords, etc.) in your reminders, since data "at rest" is not necessarily encrypted on the free tier.

// Installation & access key

iPhone: open in Safari → Share button → "Add to Home Screen" → open the app from the Home Screen → tap "Turn on notifications".

Android / desktop: open the link, and add it to your home screen if you like. Alerts arrive right in the browser.

Your access key is your only login. To use Husk on another device, copy the key at the bottom of the app and paste it there. If you clear your browser data or lose the key, you lose access to the list — there is no password recovery.
