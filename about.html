About me

I hold a degree in early childhood education and have extensive experience in the kindergarten sector, where I currently work as a pedagogical leader and HSE coordinator. In this role, I am responsible for leading the educational work in my department, as well as ensuring that health, safety, and information security standards are upheld across the entire organization — a responsibility I take very seriously.


A belief that people deserve to be protected, seen, and respected for who they are runs as a common thread through everything I do. It was this conviction that drew me into cybersecurity — a field I am now studying with great enthusiasm. I aspire to help protect individuals, privacy, and critical societal infrastructure in a world where digital threats are continuously growing.


I am driven by curiosity and a strong desire to grow, learn, and understand connections. To me, professional development is not simply about building competence — it is about becoming a better version of myself, and about being able to contribute more meaningfully to the people I serve.


When I am not immersed in security or education, I find my balance in nature. A hike in the forest, a night in the mountains, or quiet moments by a fishing lake recharge my energy and offer perspective. Camera in hand, I often capture moments along the way — from sweeping landscapes to the small details that are easily overlooked.
